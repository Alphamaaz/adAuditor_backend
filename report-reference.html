Findings in detail

The evidence, finding by finding

3.1 · Tuesdays are quietly burning $3,562

The Tuesday day-of-week segment spent $14,148 for 73 conversions — a $194 CPA against the $145 account baseline. The sample is large (72% waste share), so this is a recurring pattern, not noise. The budget governor keeps steering spend into the day regardless of how it converts.

EvidenceValue
Tuesday segment spend$14,148
Tuesday conversions73
Tuesday CPA vs baseline$194 vs $145
Sample confidenceHigh (72% waste share)
Excess spend above baseline efficiency$3,562

How to fix it

  1. Confirm the Tuesday underperformance in the platform UI for the current reporting cycle.
  2. Apply ad-schedule bid reductions (start at −20%) to Tuesday on the affected Display campaigns.
  3. Reallocate the freed budget to days converting at or below the $145 baseline.

3.2 · Your brand name is undefended

None of the 27 audited campaigns target branded keywords. The account is built around Display — so when high-intent buyers search your name, competitors can intercept them at low cost. In your matched B2B SaaS peer set, essentially every account runs brand protection. This is the cheapest, highest-converting traffic available to you, and right now anyone can buy it.

How to fix it

  1. Create a dedicated branded Search campaign with exact and phrase match variants of your name.
  2. Budget for ≥90% impression share on brand queries.
  3. Monitor competitor presence on your brand terms.

3.3 · 82% of spend rides on 2 campaigns

Spend has pooled into 2 of 6 active campaigns, all with similar Display targeting. If either destabilises — a policy flag, audience fatigue, a placement shift — most of the account's volume goes with it. This may be deliberate; if so, it should be a documented decision rather than accumulated drift.

How to fix it

  1. Document whether the concentration is deliberate strategy or accumulated drift.
  2. Plan diversification across campaign types, audiences, and keyword themes.

3.4 · 1,188 keywords with zero impressions

Every active keyword in the account — 1,188 of 1,188 — received zero impressions this period. This is a symptom of the Display-only structure (the keywords never enter an auction), not a bidding failure. The clutter adds noise to diagnostics and will hide genuine Quality Score problems if Search is activated later.

How to fix it

  1. Filter the keyword list by status: "Below first page bid" and "Low search volume."
  2. Bulk-pause labelled low-volume keywords.
  3. Keep terms intended for future Search activation in a separate paused campaign.

Method

Benchmarks, confidence & assumptions

  • Benchmarks. No CPA/ROAS targets are declared in the profile, so findings are anchored to your own $145 platform baseline and a matched same-size B2B SaaS peer set. CTR sits within 0.7% and CPC within 5.1% of the peer band — overall efficiency is effectively tied with peers.
  • Data quality. Conversion Tracking scores 100/100. No tracking issues were detected, so the CPA figures in this report are reliable for the spend they describe.
  • Confidence. The Tuesday finding rests on a sufficient sample (high confidence). The brand-protection and concentration findings are structural detections; their dollar impact is not quantifiable from available data and is labelled as such — this report does not invent figures.
  • Trends. No prior audit exists for comparison, so trend direction cannot be quoted this cycle. From the next audit onward, the day-of-week and concentration findings become trackable deltas.
